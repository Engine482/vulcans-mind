# Люстерко (Lusterko) — public project note

Lusterko is a small, self-funded MVP exploring how lightweight digital screening can help service members and civilians notice changes in their own mental state in the context of the war in Ukraine. It is intentionally narrow: it is not a clinical decision-support tool, not a therapy product, and not a diagnostic system. The name "Люстерко" means "little mirror" — the framing is reflection, not assessment.

## Scope

The project focuses on short, validated self-report instruments adapted for repeated use, an honest distinction between screening and diagnosis, accessible language in Ukrainian and English, and a calm, non-alarming user experience. Long-term symptom monitoring, integration with formal medical records, and any kind of remote diagnosis are explicitly out of scope. The product does not claim to replace a clinician.

## Privacy and safety

By design, the application avoids storing identifying personal data beyond what is strictly necessary for the experience. It does not collect free-text journal entries that resemble medical history, does not share data with third parties for advertising or analytics beyond aggregate operational metrics, and does not represent results as a diagnosis. Ambiguous or elevated screening results route the user to general information about professional support rather than to automated advice.

## Status

Lusterko is at MVP stage and has been deployed for early demonstration purposes. It is not a generally available consumer product. Maturity, adoption, and clinical validation claims are deliberately understated; any future expansion beyond this MVP would require external review, and that review has not happened.

## Relationship to Vulcan's Mind

Vulcan's Mind, the chatbot on motornyi.com, can describe Lusterko at this public level as part of Volodymyr's project context. It is not the Lusterko product itself, has no access to Lusterko user data, and does not perform screening. Questions that ask Lusterko-style screening questions through the chatbot are routed to the project's normal medical-safety guardrails rather than answered as if the chatbot were the screening tool.
